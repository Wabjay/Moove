How do I pay for my Fair car?

Fair makes it easy to set up payments right in the Fair app. You have the option to pay by credit or debit card or link directly to your bank account.

What type of payment methods do you accept?

Fair accepts the following payment methods for recurring payments:

  • ACH (Automatic Clearing House)
  • Debit card
  • Credit card
  • Apple Pay (Apple OS)
  • Google Pay (Android OS)

What is an ACH payment?

An ACH payment is a direct payment that is withdrawn from your bank account. You can set up ACH in the Fair app by linking directly to your bank account. Once you set up ACH, your recurring payment will automatically deduct on your payment date.

Am I required to set up automatic payments?

Yes. Fair requires you to set up digital automatic payments, which you can do easily through the Fair app with a bank account, debit card or credit card with a valid U.S. address.

What will I owe upfront?

At checkout, you’ll make your down payment, your first monthly payment, and any add-ons you’ve selected in the app, such as extra miles or excess wear & tear protection. In some cases, you may also be required to make an additional refundable security deposit.

What is the Down Payment?

Our down payment is an upfront fee due at signing and before driving away. The down payment generally covers the costs of initial documentation, registration and licensing fees and has the effect of reducing the cost of monthly payments for the Fair program, which comes with routine maintenance, limited warranty, and roadside assistance included. For our 2 year and 3 year leases, the down payment is the capitalized cost reduction as in a common lease. The cap cost reduction is an upfront payment that has the effect of reducing the monthly lease payments for a car.

What does the refundable security deposit cover?

The refundable security deposit you may be required to make upfront will be applied toward any amounts due when you return your Fair car, such as tolls & citations, mileage overages or excess wear & tear. If nothing is due, you’ll get your security deposit back in full.

When is my first monthly payment due?

Your first monthly payment is due upfront when you sign your contracts, along with your down payment, any refundable security deposit, and any add-ons you may have purchased, like additional mileage or excess wear & tear protection.

When will my following monthly payments be due?

Your monthly payment date is determined by when you get your Fair car. Once your monthly payment date is locked in, it will remain the same for every month. For example, if you picked up your car (or the car was delivered) on June 6th, your next payment will be on July 6th, then August 6th, etc.

If you picked up your car (or the car was delivered) on the 29th, 30th, or 31st of a month, your monthly payment date would be pushed to the 1st of the month and remain on the 1st of each following month. For example, if you picked up (or the car was delivered) on June 29th, your next payment would be August 1st, then September 1st, etc.

How much is the late charge?

You may incur a late charge for any payment that is not received within 10 days after your due date. The late charge will be $25 or 5% of the amount due, whichever is lower.

I'm having trouble making the down payment, security deposit or first monthly payment. Why am I getting an error?

If you're getting an error message, check with your bank or credit card company to make sure:

  • The dollar amount of available funds matches the total required for your down payment
  • Pending deposits to your bank account have cleared for available use
  • Fair is authorized on the account to withdraw this amount
  • Your card isn’t being declined for any other reason

If you've called your bank or credit card company about these issues and still need further assistance, please don't hesitate to chat with or call us in the app to figure things out.

My bank isn't listed when I try to connect an account. What else can I use?

If your bank isn't listed, you can authorize your payments using a credit or debit card.

How does billing work if I return my Fair car in the middle of a billing period?

If you paid your first monthly payment at checkout, you are billed in advance so you will receive a prorated credit on your final bill for the unused days.

If you did not pay your first monthly payment until a month after receiving your Fair car, then you are likely being billed in arrears. After returning your car, you will receive a bill that is prorated to reflect the number of days you had the car since the close of the previous billing cycle.

If you’re unsure whether you’re billed in advance or arrears, you can call or chat with a Fair representative through the app. We're available Monday-Friday from 7a.m.-5 p.m. Pacific, and on Saturday from 7 a.m.-4 p.m. We're not available on Sundays. You can also email us at support@fair.com.

Why did I receive an email regarding a sales tax update?

From time to time, there are changes in the sales tax that can affect your monthly payment. These changes affect drivers. Your notification is just a friendly reminder to keep you apprised of the latest information regarding any changes in taxes.

I received a citation, toll or ticket. What should I do?

You are responsible for any citation, toll or ticket that you incur while driving a Fair car. We recommend that you pay your citation, toll or ticket before the due date in order to avoid incurring additional fees. Please note that in order to renew registration, all citations and tolls must be paid in full. If you have other questions regarding a toll or citation, please contact us at tolls@fair.com

What happens if I don’t pay my citation or toll ticket?

If you don’t pay a citation or toll, Fair will receive a notice of the violation and will make the initial payment on behalf of the customer to avoid incurring additional fees or creating interruptions in your subscription.

Once the fee (including any penalties from the issuing authority, if applicable) has been paid by Fair, that amount will be added to your outstanding balance in the Fair app.
